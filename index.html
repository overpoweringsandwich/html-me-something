Sell Yah Later
- A Coding Story

The Beginning

Back when I was a lad(about 13 years old), I began building computers with my friends for gaming. On the weekends we would setup LANs in my friends basement. After a while we began to understand how to manipulate different features of the game. We began experimenting with making adjustments to our systems, maintaining them etc. After learning more about hardware, we became more interested in how software worked. My friend David even started becoming proficient in C++. I dabbled in python and visual basic, we were enjoying what we could do.

Computers & Real Life

Eventually we stopped spending as much time learning about computers, and just casually gamed. We became busier with things such as college and careers, so our energy was elsewhere. I didn't think that I would get into computers as a career, and chose business as my major. Although, computers were always still interesting to me, so I still decided to get into the software industry.

Post-College

After I finished my degree, I looked to the software companies for sales and consulting positions. I quickly learned I didn't find the sales process interesting, nor did I see myself doing this as a career. I started to think about what else I might enjoy doing, and computer programming was one of those ideas. After looking into different possibilities, I put together a plan. Thankfully, that plan landed me at LaunchCode.

Present Day

Today I'm a student at LaunchCode, am enjoying what I'm learning to do, and looking forward to career opportunities. So far it's been awesome!
